The indipetae are letters written by Jesuits to their Superior General in application for the extra-European missions, usually called the "Indies." In Latin, applying for the Indies was petere Indias; from this are derived the expressions indipetae (the letters) and indipeti (the applicants). After the Suppression (1773) and the Restoration (1814) of the Society of Jesus, the tradition of writing these petitions continued. This database collects both the indipetae of the Old (1540-1773) and the New Society (1814).
